Digital Footprints, Local Storage & Transparency

Last Updated: January 20, 2026

1. The "Minimalist" Philosophy

At Jaiho Digital, we believe in keeping software lightweight—and that applies to our tracking too. We do not use aggressive advertising cookies or sell your browsing history. We use data storage technologies strictly to make our applications function correctly.

2. Cookies vs. Local Storage

Modern web applications function differently than old websites. We use two types of storage:

  • Cookies: Small files sent to your browser from our server. Used primarily for "Sessions" (keeping you logged in).
  • Local Storage: A database inside your browser. Apps like Kernel Pulse and Jaiho IP use this to save your preferences (like "Dark Mode" or "Probe History") directly on your device without sending it to our servers.

3. Categorization of Data

Category Purpose
Essential Required for Visio AI and Dev Link to run. These manage user sessions and prevent the app from crashing.
Functional Used by Kernel Pulse to save your last scanned IP or API settings locally on your machine.
Analytics Anonymous metrics (e.g., "50 visitors from India") to help us optimize server load. We do not track individual identities.

4. Third-Party Integrations

Our ecosystem connects several powerful platforms. When you use specific tools, these providers may set their own cookies:

  • Hugging Face Spaces: When using Radiant AI or NGIBS, Hugging Face manages the authentication cookies.
  • Streamlit Community Cloud: Hosting for our data apps requires functional cookies for performance.
  • GitHub: If you use "Login with GitHub" for any future Dev tools.

5. Managing Your Preferences

Since we respect your autonomy, you have full control:

  • Browser Settings: You can block all cookies via Chrome/Firefox/Safari settings. Note: This may break the login features of Visio AI.
  • Clear Local Storage: You can wipe the memory of Kernel Pulse by clearing your browser's cache.
  • Incognito Mode: Forces all cookies to be deleted immediately upon closing the window.

6. Updates

We may update this policy as we deploy new "Intelligent Labs" modules. Check back here for the latest protocols.
